Detailed Policy Sections

Comprehensive guidelines for copyright, licensing, and reuse of SOLAV Journal content.

SOLAV Journal is committed to promoting open, transparent, and responsible dissemination of scholarly research. This policy explains the ownership of copyright, licensing terms, and permitted uses of published content.

All articles are published to maximize accessibility, reuse, and long-term preservation.

Authors retain full copyright to their published work.

By submitting and publishing with SOLAV Journal, authors grant the Journal a non-exclusive license to:

  • Publish, reproduce, and distribute the work
  • Identify itself as the original publisher
  • Archive and preserve the content
  • Authorize third-party indexing and archiving services

This agreement does not restrict authors from using their work in future publications, repositories, or academic activities.

SOLAV Journal operates under a full open-access model.

  • All published articles are immediately and permanently available online without subscription or access fees.
  • No embargo periods apply.

Unless otherwise stated, all articles are published under the:

Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International License (CC BY 4.0)

This license permits:

  • Sharing and redistribution
  • Adaptation and transformation
  • Commercial and non-commercial reuse

Provided that appropriate credit is given to the original authors and source.

License details: https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/4.0/

Authors retain the right to:

  • Deposit published versions in institutional or subject repositories
  • Share articles on personal or academic websites
  • Use content in teaching and presentations
  • Reuse material in future works
  • Distribute copies to colleagues

No permission from the Journal is required for these uses.

Readers and third parties may:

  • Read, download, copy, distribute, and reuse content
  • Translate or adapt articles
  • Use content for research, education, and commercial purposes

In accordance with CC BY 4.0 requirements.

Proper attribution must always be provided.

All reuse must include:

  • Author(s) name(s)
  • Article title
  • Journal name
  • Publication year
  • License information

Example attribution format:

Author(s). (Year). Title. SOLAV Journal. URL. Licensed under CC BY 4.0.

Authors are responsible for obtaining permission to use third-party material not covered by CC BY.

  • Such material must be clearly identified in the article.
  • Unauthorized use may result in correction or removal.

SOLAV Journal ensures long-term preservation through:

  • Zenodo archiving
  • External backup repositories
  • Institutional preservation systems
  • Redundant server storage

Published content remains permanently accessible.

Upon acceptance, authors must complete a publishing agreement confirming:

  • Originality of the work
  • Copyright ownership
  • License acceptance
  • Ethical compliance

Failure to complete the agreement may delay publication.

In exceptional cases, alternative licenses may be considered upon request prior to publication.

Such requests are evaluated individually by the Editorial Office.

Violation of licensing terms may result in:

  • Article withdrawal
  • Correction notices
  • Legal action (if necessary)
  • Restriction of future submissions

This policy is reviewed regularly to reflect legal and scholarly developments.

Updates are published on the Journal website.

For copyright and licensing inquiries:

Publishing Office
Email: publishing@solav.me

Key Benefits of Our Licensing Model

Author Freedom

Authors retain full copyright while granting broad dissemination rights to the journal. No restrictions on future use of your own work.

Maximum Reach

CC BY licensing ensures your work can be read, shared, and built upon globally without barriers. Increases citations and impact.

Legal Clarity

Clear, standardized licensing eliminates confusion about what users can do with published content. Protects both authors and users.

Permanent Access

Multiple archiving strategies ensure your work remains accessible for the long term, independent of journal platform changes.

Commercial Friendly

CC BY allows commercial reuse, enabling textbooks, translations, and other commercial applications that extend your reach.

Community Building

Open licensing fosters collaboration and knowledge sharing, accelerating scientific progress and innovation across disciplines.
